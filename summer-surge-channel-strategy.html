Strategic Overview

Summer is the critical enrollment window for Radical Reading. Parents are thinking about their children's year ahead, school planning is visible, and—critically—the reading window for next grade entry is closing.

This playbook defines how we move parents from awareness → consideration → enrollment across email, web, social, and paid channels. The goal is to make “now” feel urgent without fear-mongering, position SURGE as the right intervention for emerging readers, and showcase Lark’s clinical authority as the differentiator.

Core Messaging for Summer

The Window Is Open (But Not For Long)

The reading window for Grade 1 and Grade 2 readiness is closing. Intervention now shapes their entire reading trajectory. Not in September. Now.

SURGE Is Intervention, Not Tutoring

SURGE is clinical, structured, evidence-based literacy intervention designed for emerging readers who need the right foundation at the right moment.

Lark Barker, Clinician

This is not generic tutoring. This is directed by Lark Barker, a former TDSB literacy specialist, Right to Read inquiry contributor, and structured literacy expert. Clinical oversight at every step.

Email Channel Strategy

Segment: Parents of Kindergarten → Grade 1 Children

Message: “The window for Grade 2 reading success is now. Your child’s neuroplasticity for reading is at peak capacity. We are the experts. Let’s use it.”

  • Email 1: The Reading Window (education, urgency)
  • Email 2: Why Intervention Works Now (science + case study)
  • Email 3: SURGE Program Details + Pricing
  • Email 4: Lark Barker's Background + Guarantees
  • Email 5: Scarcity (limited summer spots, intake closing)

Segment: Parents Expressing Concern About Reading

Message: “Your instinct that something needs attention is right. And you’re not too late.”

  • Email 1: “Is My Child Struggling?” self-assessment
  • Email 2: Why Early Help Changes Everything
  • Email 3: SURGE Program as Clinical Intervention
  • Email 4: Lark's Expertise + Methodology
  • Email 5: Book a Consult (conversion focus)

Social Channel Strategy

Thematic Buckets (Rotate weekly)

  • The Window: Brain science facts. Why summer matters. Neuroplasticity and the closing window.
  • Barriers: “Most schools don’t catch this until Grade 4.” Why waiting costs years.
  • Authority: Lark's credentials. Right to Read work. Clinical methodology explainers.
  • Proof: Parent testimonials (with consent). Before/after progress examples.

Web Strategy

Landing Page: “SURGE for Summer”

  • Hero: “The window closes after summer. Now is the time.”
  • Social proof: Parent stories. Testimonials from prior years.
  • Lark section: Who she is, why she matters, what makes SURGE different.
  • Program details: Duration, methodology, what to expect.
  • CTA: Book a consultation. Limited summer spots.
  • Pricing: Clear, transparent, no surprises.

Paid Media Strategy

Google Search Ads

Target: "summer reading programs," "reading intervention," "dyslexia help," "struggling reader," "reading tutor near me"

Message: Clinical intervention. Lark's expertise. Limited summer availability.

Social Ads (Meta)

Audiences: Parents of K-2 children. Engagement-based lookalikes. Website visitors (retargeting).

Creative: Parent testimonials. Window explainers. Lark credibility. Scarcity (summer spots).

LinkedIn Ads

Target: Educators. School administrators. Parents in professional networks.

Message: Evidence-based. Lark's research background. Structured literacy authority.

Conversion Milestones

  • Awareness: 10K impressions across channels by end of May
  • Consideration: 500 click-throughs to landing page by mid-June
  • Evaluation: 100 consultation bookings by end of June
  • Enrollment: 40-60 SURGE enrollments by mid-July

Post-Enrollment

Once enrolled, parents enter a communication cadence about their child's progress, brain development, what to expect week-by-week, and how to support learning at home.
