Pivot Point — Between P4 & P5
Category Reframe / Objection Intercept
The hinge of the arc — do not skip
⚠︎ By the end of P4 the parent accepts the stakes — but their escape hatch is still open: "If something goes wrong, we'll just get a tutor." This move closes that hatch before P5 opens. It is not a sales argument. It is a category redefinition. Radical Reading is not a better version of tutoring. It is a different decision, made at a different moment, for a different reason.
Canonical delivery
"Tutoring is what you do after reading becomes a problem. Radical Reading is what you do instead."
Alternate deployments — headline-ready
  • "'Wait and see' is a strategy. So is this. One of them ends in a tutor. The other ends in a reader."
  • "Most parents find us two years too late. Not because they didn't care — because nobody told them the window closes. Now you know." ↳ Use with care — powerful for IGNITE/SURGE, risks shame for REWIRE. See track notes below.
Track sensitivity
IGNITE SURGE Deploy with confidence. Flatters the proactive parent identity — this is the line that makes them feel seen. Lead with the canonical delivery.
BRIDGE Use with warmth. Reframe as validation: "You caught it before tutoring became the only option." Pivot quickly — don't linger on what could have been.
REWIRE Handle carefully or skip entirely. These parents are already in triage. Emphasising prevention adds shame, not motivation. Move to P5 faster.
What this is not: a tutoring takedown. Don't linger or turn it competitive. The point is made in one line — let it land and move. The contrast is the argument; elaboration dilutes it.
