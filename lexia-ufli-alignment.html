LEVELS 2–5  ·  Kindergarten  ·  A Day at the Beach · A Snow Day in the City · The Amazon Rainforest · The Scottish Cliffs
Levels 2–5 PA Blending & Segmenting Syllables & Sounds All lessons (PA warm-up) Phoneme blending & segmentingDaily warm-up routine in every UFLI lesson ●●● Direct alignment. UFLI's phoneme warm-up routine targets blending and segmenting every session. Use Elkonin boxes for concrete, multisensory support.
Levels 2–5 PA Beginning Sounds · Ending Sounds ~L. 1–14 (PA warm-up) Phoneme isolationInitial and final phoneme identification ●●● PA warm-up in early UFLI lessons explicitly targets first and last sound isolation. Pair with Elkonin boxes (tap first/last box only).
Levels 2–5 PA Short & Long Vowel Sounds · Manipulating Sounds All lessons (PA warm-up) Vowel sound discrimination & phoneme substitutionIntegrated throughout PA warm-up routine ●●○ Vowel discrimination is built through word reading practice. Phoneme substitution (e.g., "change /a/ to /i/ in 'cat' — what word?") is explicit in UFLI's PA routine.
Levels 2–5 PH Letter-Sound CorrespondenceIndividual consonants and short vowels ~L. 1–7 Single letter-sound correspondencesConsonants + short vowels a, i, o, u, e ●●● Strong, direct alignment. UFLI Lessons 1–7 build letter-sound knowledge systematically. Use UFLI sound-spelling cards for each letter introduced.
Levels 2–5 PH Letter Names · Alphabetizing ~Pre-lessons, L. 1–7 Letter naming & alphabet knowledgeEmbedded in early lessons; no standalone alphabetizing lesson ●○○ VerifyUFLI doesn't have a standalone alphabetizing lesson. Levels 2–5 PH Short Vowels in CVC WordsAll five short vowels ~L. 8–14 CVC decoding & encoding — short a, i, o, u, eOne vowel introduced per lesson with word lists and connected text ●●● Strong alignment. If a student is stuck on a specific short vowel, pull the corresponding UFLI lesson (e.g., short /e/ confusion → UFLI Lesson ~13–14). Use Elkonin boxes for encoding practice.
Levels 2–5 FL High-Frequency Sight WordsAutomaticity with foundational concepts Every lesson (Trick Words) Irregular "trick words"3–4 new words introduced per lesson; cumulative review throughout ●●● UFLI calls these "trick words" and introduces a small set each lesson with explicit irregular pattern explanation. Directly mirrors Lexia's HF word focus.
Levels 2–5 VC Vocabulary Concepts · Advanced Adjectives — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementVocabulary instruction is outside UFLI's scope. Use picture word walls, oral language routines, and explicit concept vocabulary activities.

LEVELS 6–9  ·  Grade 1  ·  A Day in Paris · The African Serengeti · The South Pole · The Egyptian Desert
Levels 6–9 PA Manipulating Sounds (substitutions)Short & long vowel discrimination All lessons (PA warm-up) Phoneme substitutionSwap vowels/consonants to make new words; daily routine ●●● PA warm-up in every UFLI lesson. Phoneme substitution ("change /a/ to /e/ in 'hat'") is explicit. Increase vowel complexity as lessons progress (short to long vowels).
Levels 6–9 PH Digraphssh, ch, th, wh, ck, qu, ng ~L. 15–22 Consonant digraphs — decoding & encodingsh (L.15), ch (L.16), th (L.17), wh (L.18), ck (L.19), qu (L.20), ng (L.21–22) ●●● Strong alignment. UFLI dedicates one lesson per digraph. When a student stalls on a specific digraph in Lexia, pull the matching UFLI lesson for explicit re-instruction + decodable text practice.
Levels 6–9 PH Consonant BlendsInitial (l-, r-, s-blends) & final blends — note: implicit in Lexia S&S ~L. 23–34 Initial and final consonant clustersl-blends (L.23–24), r-blends (L.25–26), s-blends (L.27–29), final blends (L.30–34) ●●● VerifyBlends are not explicitly named in the Lexia S&S overview but are a core Grade 1 phonics skill taught in this range. Pull the specific blend cluster causing breakdown (e.g., student dropping /l/ in "bl-" → UFLI L.~23).
Levels 6–9 PH Welded / Glued Sounds-all, -am, -an; -ang/-ing/-ong/-ung; -ank/-ink/-onk/-unk ~L. 35–40 Welded (glued) sound patterns-all, -am, -an (L.35–36); nasal -ng patterns (L.37–38); nasal -nk patterns (L.39–40) ●●● Strong alignment. UFLI explicitly teaches these as "welded sounds" (phonemes that blend so tightly they must be learned as units). Use UFLI word lists and Elkonin boxes with the nasal patterns marked.
Levels 6–9 PH Easily Reversible Lettersb, d, p discrimination ~L. 1–7 Letter formation & discriminationEmbedded in letter introduction; not a standalone lesson ●○○ No dedicated UFLI b/d/p reversal lesson. Use verbal cues ("b is a bat then a ball"), tactile letter formation, and UFLI sound-spelling card visual exposure. This is primarily an automaticity/orthographic mapping issue.
Levels 6–9 PH Word FamiliesRime-based patterns ~L. 8–22 Rime patterns within CVC and digraph words-at, -an, -it, -op, -ish, etc. — implicit in UFLI word work ●●○ UFLI doesn't use the term "word families" but builds them through CVC and digraph word lists. The UFLI word sort activity can be used to explicitly highlight rime patterns for students who benefit from this lens.
Levels 6–9 PH Contractions — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementContractions are not a phonics focus in UFLI Foundations. Address as a spelling convention / sight word activity. Contractions appear in decodable texts and can be noted and discussed in context.
Levels 6–9 PH Syllable Type: ClosedCVC, CVCC — short vowel ~L. 1–22 (implicit); ~L. 91–96 (named explicitly) Closed syllable patternFoundation of all early UFLI lessons; named in multisyllabic section ●●● Closed syllable IS the structural basis of all UFLI Lessons 1–22. The term is explicitly introduced in the multisyllabic section (~L. 91–96). For decoding breakdown: reinforce via any early UFLI lesson with word card work.
Levels 6–9 PH Syllable Type: OpenCV — long vowel ~L. 91–96 Open syllable (CV → long vowel sound)Introduced in multisyllabic word reading section ●●○ Open syllable is introduced in UFLI's multisyllabic section. For students stuck on open syllables at this level, jump ahead to UFLI L.~91–96 for explicit instruction and contrast with closed syllable.
Levels 6–9 PH Syllable Type: Silent e (CVCe)Long vowel with final silent e ~L. 41–50 Magic/silent e — long vowel CVCe patternsa_e (L.41–42), i_e (L.43–44), o_e (L.45–46), u_e (L.47–48), e_e (L.49) ●●● Strong alignment. UFLI dedicates 1–2 lessons per CVCe vowel. If a student stalls on a specific long vowel (e.g., i_e), pull that UFLI lesson for explicit re-instruction with decodable text and word work.
Levels 6–9 FL High-Frequency Sight Words · Sentence Structure Every lesson (Trick Words) Cumulative trick word bankRunning list of ~200+ irregular words by program end ●●● UFLI trick word list closely mirrors common HF word lists (Fry, Dolch). Sentence structure is supported through UFLI connected decodable text reading at each level.
Levels 6–9 VC Categorizing & Associations · Multiple Meaning Words · Parts of Speech — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementVocabulary instruction is outside UFLI's scope. Use semantic mapping, word sorts by meaning, and explicit vocabulary routines from a dedicated program.
Levels 6–9 CP Listening & Reading Comprehension · Text Structure · Comprehension Strategies — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementUFLI decodable texts can support basic retelling/sequencing, but structured comprehension strategy instruction requires separate resources.
LEVELS 10–12  ·  Grade 2  ·  An English Garden · The Swiss Alps · A Russian Circus
Levels 10–12 PA Manipulating Sounds (additions & deletions) All lessons (PA warm-up) Phoneme addition & deletionAdvanced manipulation in PA warm-up routine ●●○ Use the PA warm-up of any UFLI lesson to practice phoneme addition ("say 'cat,' add /s/ to the end") and deletion ("say 'stop' without /s/"). Complexity scales with the lesson's phonics focus.
Levels 10–12 PH Vowel Combinations (Vowel Teams)ai/ay, ee/ea, oa/ow, oo, ie/igh, ue/ew ~L. 51–68 Long vowel digraphs & teamsai/ay (~L.51–53), ee/ea (~L.54–56), oa/ow/oe (~L.57–59), oo/ue/ew (~L.60–63), ie/igh (~L.64–65) ●●● Strong alignment. Pull the specific vowel team lesson matching the student's breakdown. Each UFLI lesson isolates one pattern with dedicated word lists and a decodable text. Particularly useful for ai vs. ay and ee vs. ea confusions.
Levels 10–12 PH Vowel r / R-controlled Vowelsar, or, er, ir, ur ~L. 69–75 R-controlled vowel patternsar (~L.69), or (~L.70), er/ir/ur (~L.71–73) ●●● Strong alignment. UFLI treats each r-controlled pattern in its own lesson. If a student confuses er/ir/ur (all pronounced /er/), pull all three lessons for side-by-side comparison and word sorting.
Levels 10–12 PH Diphthongsoi/oy, ou/ow, au/aw (under vowel combinations) ~L. 76–83 Diphthongs & other complex vowel patternsoi/oy (~L.76–77), ou/ow (~L.78–79), au/aw (~L.80–81) ●●● Strong alignment. Pull the lesson for the specific diphthong causing breakdown. oi/oy and ou/ow are frequent confusion points. Note that ou/ow can represent multiple sounds — UFLI addresses this with pattern rules.
Levels 10–12 PH Complex Consonant Patterns-tch, -dge; silent letters (kn, wr, mb, ph) ~L. 82–88 -tch/-dge spelling rules; silent letter combinations-tch/-dge (~L.82–83); kn/wr/mb (~L.84–86); ph (~L.87–88) ●●● Good alignment. UFLI teaches -tch/-dge as position-based spelling rules (after short vowel). Silent letter patterns addressed individually. Use UFLI word sorts for pattern discrimination.
Levels 10–12 PH Hard & Soft c and gce/ci/cy = /s/; ge/gi/gy = /j/ ~L. 84–87 Soft c and soft g — positional spelling rulesBefore e, i, y: c = /s/, g = /j/ ●●● Good alignment. UFLI covers soft c and g as position-based rules with word lists. Emphasize the "before e, i, y" rule. Word sort (hard vs. soft) is highly effective for this pattern.
Levels 10–12 PH Consonant-le Syllable Type-ble, -tle, -ple, -dle, -fle, -kle, -zle ~L. 91–95 Consonant + le final syllableIn multisyllabic word reading section ●●○ Covered in UFLI's multisyllabic section. Pair with syllable division lessons so students can identify where to split (e.g., sim-ple, ta-ble). Reinforce with word sort by ending (-ble vs. -tle vs. -ple).
Levels 10–12 PH Rules for Syllable DivisionVC/CV, V/CV, VCC/CV patterns ~L. 91–96 Syllable division strategiesVCCV and VCV splitting rules for multisyllabic decoding ●●● Strong alignment. UFLI teaches explicit syllable division. Have students mark the split on UFLI word cards, then decode each syllable type. Reinforces the why behind long-word breakdown.
Levels 10–12 PH Spelling Generalizations & Rules-ck after short vowel; floss rule; doubling consonants ~L. 19–22, 82–90 Spelling rules embedded in pattern instruction-ck after short vowel (L.~19–22); -tch/-dge (L.~82–83); doubling in suffix section ●●○ UFLI teaches spelling generalizations as part of each pattern lesson, not as standalone rules. Use UFLI's "why do we spell it this way?" discussion and comparison charts for the most common rules students encounter.
Levels 10–12 PH Irregular Plurals & Verbsfeet, mice, went, said, etc. — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementIrregular morphology is not addressed in UFLI Foundations. Use explicit grammar/morphology instruction and sight-word style practice for the most common irregular forms.
Levels 10–12 SA Simple Suffixes-s, -es, -ed, -ing, -er, -est (inflectional) ~L. 93–98 Inflectional suffix decoding & encoding-ed, -ing, -s/-es, -er, -est with base words and spelling rules ●●○ UFLI covers inflectional endings with attention to spelling rules (1-1-1 doubling, drop-e, change y). When a student miscodes an ending, pair with the corresponding spelling rule lesson.
Levels 10–12 SA Prefixesun-, re- (basic derivational) ~L. 97–100 Basic prefix introductionun-, re-, pre-, dis- with meaning ●○○ UFLI introduces a small set of high-frequency prefixes in the final lessons. For deeper prefix work or additional prefixes, supplement with explicit morphology instruction.
Levels 10–12 FL Timed Silent Reading (Paragraph Level) · Modeled Prosody Decodable readers (all levels) Connected decodable text readingUFLI decodable readers matched to each lesson's phonics scope ●●○ UFLI decodable readers provide connected text practice for building automaticity. For prosody specifically, use repeated reading with explicit teacher modeling — this is beyond the UFLI lesson structure itself.
Levels 10–12 VC Synonyms & Antonyms · Similes & Metaphors · Parts of Speech — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementNo UFLI equivalent. Use explicit word relationship activities, figurative language study, and vocabulary routines from a dedicated program.
LEVELS 13–15  ·  Grade 3  ·  The Indian Rainforest · A Japanese Garden · A Journey Through China
Levels 13–15 SA Prefix & Suffix MeaningsDerivational affixes ~L. 97–100 (basic only) Basic affix meaningsun- (not), re- (again), -ful, -less, -ness — small set covered ●○○ VerifyUFLI Foundations ends with a small set of high-frequency affixes. Derivational morphology at Grade 3+ requires significant supplementation. Consider morpheme-based word study (Words Their Way, Vocabulary Through Morphemes).
Levels 13–15 SA Spelling Rules for Adding Affixes1-1-1 doubling, drop-e, change y to i ~L. 93–100 Affix spelling rules for inflectional endingsDoubling, drop-e, and y→i rules covered in suffix section ●●○ UFLI covers spelling rules for adding inflectional suffixes (~L.93–98). Derivational suffix spelling rules (e.g., -tion, -sion, -ous) require supplementation beyond UFLI's scope.
Levels 13–15 SA Word Analysis Strategies · Morphological Awareness · Context Clues — Beyond UFLI Foundations scope  —  SupplementMorphological analysis strategies and context clue use are beyond UFLI Foundations. At this level, transition to morphology-focused resources (Words Their Way Within Word, Wilson Word Study, or structured word inquiry).
Levels 13–15 FL Rhyme Scheme · Intonation, Stress & Phrasing · Prosody — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementFluency prosody at this level is not addressed in UFLI. Use Readers Theater, phrase-cued reading, or repeated reading with teacher modeling and explicit feedback on expression.
Levels 13–15 VC Idioms · Analogies · Academic Vocabulary · Word Relationships · Connectives — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementAll vocabulary instruction at this level requires dedicated resources. No UFLI equivalent for figurative language, academic vocabulary tiers, analogical reasoning, or discourse connectives.
Levels 13–15 CP Higher-Order Comprehension · Knowledge Building · Text Integration · Grammar for Comprehension — No UFLI equivalent  —  SupplementComprehension strategy instruction at this level is entirely outside UFLI's scope. Use structured literacy-aligned comprehension programs, close reading routines, or text-based discussion frameworks.
